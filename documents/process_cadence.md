# TPM Operating Cadence & Process Improvement Strategy

## 📉 Diagnosing Process Pitfalls: The Cost of "Invisible Risks"
[cite_start]In complex systems, risks often develop silently over weeks before turning into combined delivery crises[cite: 25]. [cite_start]If a risk is only called out when a milestone officially slips, the team loses the valuable time buffer where mitigations are cheap to implement[cite: 25].

## 🛠️ The Proposed Change: Asynchronous Telemetry
[cite_start]To maximize engineering velocity and remove status-meeting overhead, the following operational structure is established[cite: 25]:

* **Async Daily Standup:** 2-3 bullet lines per engineer via Slack/tooling. [cite_start]No disruptive live calls[cite: 25].
* [cite_start]**Targeted Cross-Team Syncs:** A 30-minute live working session held *only* between specific teams whose technical dependencies are due that week[cite: 25].
* [cite_start]**Standing External Dependency Audits:** A dedicated, weekly verification check for external vendors (Partner flags, Tax) logged even when the answer is "no update"[cite: 25]. [cite_start]Logging "No Update" two weeks in a row acts as an automatic trigger for high-level management escalation[cite: 25].

### Rituals to Eliminate:
* [cite_start]🚫 Cross-functional full-room status reporting syncs (reading out text line-by-line that is already written)[cite: 25].
* [cite_start]🚫 Process reviews and retrospectives scheduled during critical crunch/delivery weeks[cite: 25].

## 📈 Metric of Success
[cite_start]We evaluate the performance of this operational shift by tracking the **Callout Lag**[cite: 25]. [cite_start]This measures the duration of time between when an issue began trending towards "Amber" in retrospect and when it was openly communicated[cite: 25]. [cite_start]Success is defined as this lag trending down to zero over a four-week period[cite: 25].
